Absolute Batman

4.3/5⭐

The series stars a 24-year-old blue-collar civil engineer named Bruce Wayne, who operates at night as the vigilante Batman, fighting crime with his self-designed equipment and armor.

Injustice

4.6/5⭐

After the Joker tricks him first into killing both Lois Lane and his unborn child and then destroying Metropolis with a nuclear bomb, Superman kills the Joker, becomes a tyrant, and establishes a new world order named the Regime, while Batman forms an Insurgency against him.

Invincible

4.4/5⭐

Follows the coming-of-age journey of Mark Grayson, a seemingly ordinary teenager whose father, Nolan Grayson (Omni-Man), is Earth's most powerful superhero. At age 17, Mark begins to develop his own superhuman abilities — flight, super strength, super speed, stamina, and incredible durability — and takes the moniker "Invincible." But what begins as a classic teenage superhero fantasy quickly shatters into something far darker. Mark soon discovers that his father is not a benevolent alien guardian but a member of the Viltrumite Empire, a brutal, spacefaring race of conquerors who believe in manifest destiny across the cosmos. When Omni-Man reveals his true mission — to soften Earth for Viltrumite takeover — Mark is forced to confront the horrifying legacy he has inherited, setting off a sprawling, decade-spanning saga of betrayal, war, loss, growth, and ultimately redemption.

Batman: The Dark Knight Returns

4.7/5⭐

Set in a dystopian near-future, the story presents a Gotham City that has sunk into decadence and lawlessness in the ten years since a 55-year-old Bruce Wayne retired from his war on crime. Driven by an insatiable, obsessive need to fight injustice, the aging and weary Wayne dons the cape and cowl once more, returning to the streets in a blaze of fury against a new generation of criminals.

Batman: The Killing Joke

4.7/5⭐

The story provides a definitive, tragic origin for the Joker, depicted as a failed stand-up comedian and struggling family man who, after suffering "one bad day," is driven irrevocably insane. This origin is intercut with a present-day narrative in which the Joker escapes Arkham Asylum and launches a sadistic campaign to prove a philosophical point: that any man, under sufficient pressure, can be driven as mad as he is. His target is Commissioner James Gordon, Gotham's incorruptible moral center. To break Gordon, the Joker commits a horrific act that reverberates through comics history: he shoots Gordon's daughter, Barbara (Batgirl), in the spine, paralyzing her from the waist down, and then strips her, photographs her nude body, and forces her father to view the images.

Ultimate Spider-Man

4.5/5⭐

The series stars a 24-year-old blue-collar civil engineer named Bruce Wayne, who operates at night as the vigilante Batman, fighting crime with his self-designed equipment and armor.

DareDevil: Born Again

4.6/5⭐

Explores the catastrophic destruction of Matt Murdock's personal and professional life and his subsequent, painstaking journey to redemption and rebirth.

Batman: The Court Of Owls

4.5/5⭐

a chilling new addition to Batman's rogues' gallery: a secret, centuries-old cabal of Gotham City's wealthiest and most influential families who have manipulated the city's history from the shadows. Long dismissed by Bruce Wayne as a mere nursery rhyme told to frighten children, the Court proves to be terrifyingly real when its undead assassins, the Talons, begin to target him.

Absolute Superman

4.2/5⭐

a gritty, radical reinvention of the Man of Steel. Stripped of his idyllic Kansas upbringing, Kal-El grows up as an angry, persecuted immigrant. He dons advanced Kryptonian living armor and wages a relentless war to protect society's most oppressed and exploited individuals.
